3. Any certified agenda or recording of each closed (executive) session under Tex. Gov't Code § 551.103, produced with closed-session deliberative content withheld as statutorily required, but with the subject matter posted under § 551.041, the statutory authority cited for the closed session, and the members present preserved;
4. All resolutions, orders, and other formal actions adopted by the Commissioners Court, including any order signed by the County Judge under authority of the Commissioners Court;
5. The roll-call vote on every order, resolution, or other action requiring a recorded vote;
6. For any regular meeting of the Commissioners Court that did not occur on its scheduled date during the requested period: any notice of cancellation, postponement, rescheduling, or amended posting issued by the County Judge or County Clerk; any internal correspondence (memoranda, emails, text messages, or other writings) memorializing the cancellation, postponement, or rescheduling.

**CATEGORICAL PUBLIC INFORMATION, § 552.022(a)(4) AND (a)(17).** The records of voting and the names of officials in proceedings of the Commissioners Court are categorical public information under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.022(a)(4) ("the name of each official and the final record of voting on all proceedings in a governmental body"). To the extent any responsive record is also filed with or made part of any court proceeding, it is additionally public information under § 552.022(a)(17). These records are not subject to discretionary exceptions under § 552.103, § 552.108, § 552.111, or otherwise. *See* Open Records Decision Nos. 665 (2000), 664 (1999), and 177 (1977).

**OPEN MEETINGS ACT COMPLIANCE.** Minutes (or recordings) maintained under Tex. Gov't Code § 551.021 are required to be available for public inspection under § 551.022. As of the date of this request, no minutes or recordings of any Commissioners Court meeting subsequent to March 23, 2026 have been published or made publicly accessible. If responsive records do not exist for any meeting that occurred during the requested period, I request a written statement of that fact identifying each affected meeting.

**CLOSED-SESSION CONTENT.** Requestor acknowledges that the contents of certified agendas of closed sessions under § 551.103 are statutorily confidential and accessible only by court order in a proceeding under Tex. Gov't Code § 551.142. Requestor does not seek the deliberative content of any closed session. Requestor does seek the statutorily required public elements: the posted subject matter under § 551.041; the statutory authority cited for entering closed session under § 551.103; and the members present, all of which are publicly required and not within the scope of the closed-session confidentiality.

**LITIGATION-EXCEPTION REBUTTAL, § 552.103.** To the extent any agenda item or commissioner court action addressed the County's response to Requestor's pending claims, the existence and noticed subject matter of any such item are publicly required to be disclosed under the Open Meetings Act and may not be retroactively concealed via the discretionary § 552.103 exception. *See* Open Records Decision No. 588 (1991). The Open Meetings Act's notice and disclosure obligations are not waivable by reason of subsequent litigation.

**REDACTION DEFERENCE.** I acknowledge that responsive records may contain information the responding entity is required by law to withhold or redact, including without limitation closed-session deliberative content under § 551.103, personally identifying information protected under Tex. Gov't Code §§ 552.117, 552.130, and 552.147, and information specifically made confidential by statute. I request that such legally required redactions be applied and the remainder of the records be produced. If any responsive record is withheld in its entirety, I request an itemized index identifying the document, the statutory basis asserted, and a brief description.
