**PUBLIC-COURT-RECORD CATEGORY.** To the extent any responsive record has been filed with, attached to, introduced before, or otherwise made part of any court proceeding (including state habeas corpus, federal civil rights litigation, Texas Commission on Jail Standards complaint proceedings, or other administrative or judicial review), that record is public information under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.022(a)(17) and may not be withheld under § 552.108, § 552.103, or any other discretionary exception. *See* Open Records Decision Nos. 665 (2000), 664 (1999), and 177 (1977). The only exceptions that survive against subsection (a)(17) are mandatory confidentiality statutes.

**ADMINISTRATIVE NATURE OF GRIEVANCES.** Prisoner grievance records are administrative complaint-handling records, not records of the detection, investigation, or prosecution of crime. § 552.108 is therefore not a proper basis for blanket withholding of grievance records.

**LITIGATION-EXCEPTION REBUTTAL, § 552.103.** Requestor's anticipated litigation concerns specific identifiable incidents involving Requestor's spouse. The grievance records of other identified or unidentified female inmates over a five-year period are not records "of" Requestor's anticipated litigation; they are public-accountability records of an institutional pattern that exists and would exist independent of any private claim by Requestor. *See* Open Records Decision No. 588 (1991); Open Records Decision No. 551 (1990). The grievance records sought here are equally subject to disclosure if requested by a journalist, civil-rights organization, or any unrelated citizen. All responsive records will be made publicly available at **aiether.info** within seven (7) business days, independent of any pending or anticipated claim — establishing that production is for public-accountability journalism, not civil discovery.

**PREA CONFIDENTIALITY SCOPE.** Requestor acknowledges that 28 C.F.R. § 115.61 imposes federal confidentiality obligations on the substance and identity of PREA-related complaints, and that this confidentiality is mandatory and broader than victim identity alone. Requestor requests that PREA-protected substance be appropriately redacted, with the residue of each record (date received, fact of complaint, category of complaint, disposition, fact of investigation, fact of policy action) produced where redaction is possible without revealing PREA-protected identity or substance.

**REDACTION DEFERENCE.** I acknowledge that responsive records may contain information the responding entity is required by law to withhold or redact, including:

- Alleged-victim identifying information and PREA-protected complaint substance, under 28 C.F.R. § 115.61 and Tex. Code Crim. Proc. arts. 57 and 57A;
- Medical and mental-health information protected under federal HIPAA, Tex. Gov't Code § 552.101, and Tex. Health & Safety Code Ch. 611;
- Personally identifying information of officers and employees under Tex. Gov't Code §§ 552.117, 552.130, and 552.147;
- Juvenile records under Tex. Family Code Chapter 58, to the extent applicable;
- Other information specifically made confidential by statute or common-law privacy doctrine.

I request that such legally required redactions be applied and the remainder of the records be produced. Where redaction of identifying information is required, I request that the remaining substantive content of the grievance, decision, and related records be produced with the identifying information redacted. If any responsive record is withheld in its entirety, I request an itemized index identifying the document, the statutory basis asserted, and a brief description.
