**CATEGORICAL PUBLIC INFORMATION, § 552.022(a)(2).** Information concerning the name, sex, ethnicity, salary, title, and dates of employment of each named individual is categorical public information under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.022(a)(2) and may not be withheld under any discretionary exception. *See* Open Records Decision Nos. 665 (2000), 664 (1999), and 177 (1977).

**TCOLE RECORDS, TEX. OCC. CODE § 1701.452.** Form F-5 separation reports and the Personal Status Information maintained by TCOLE concerning each named individual are public records under Tex. Occ. Code § 1701.452 and related provisions. To the extent CCSO holds copies of these records, they are subject to disclosure under § 552.022(a)(17) and applicable Occupations Code provisions.

**PUBLIC-COURT-RECORD CATEGORY.** To the extent any responsive record has been filed with, attached to, introduced before, or otherwise made part of any court proceeding — including the pending criminal prosecution styled *State of Texas v.* ████████████████, Cause No. 26-045-DCCR-00041, 25th Judicial District Court of Colorado County, Texas — that record is public information under § 552.022(a)(17) and may not be withheld under § 552.108, § 552.103, § 552.102, or any other discretionary exception. *See* Open Records Decision Nos. 665 (2000), 664 (1999), and 177 (1977). The only exceptions that survive against subsection (a)(17) are mandatory confidentiality statutes.

**LITIGATION-EXCEPTION REBUTTAL, § 552.103.** The named individuals are peace officers and detention officers performing public duties at a county-operated jail. Records of their qualifications, training, discipline, and official conduct are public-accountability records, equally subject to disclosure if requested by any journalist, civil-rights organization, or unrelated citizen. *See* Open Records Decision No. 588 (1991). The records are sought for public-accountability journalism; production for civil-discovery purposes is independently available through other channels and is not the purpose of this request.

**PERSONNEL-PRIVACY BALANCING, § 552.102.** Requestor acknowledges that § 552.102 permits withholding of personnel information whose disclosure would constitute a clearly unwarranted invasion of personal privacy, subject to the balancing test articulated in *Hubert v. Harte-Hanks Tex. Newspapers*, 652 S.W.2d 546 (Tex. App.—Austin 1983, writ ref'd n.r.e.). For each category of personnel information for which CCSO contends § 552.102 supports withholding, Requestor requests that CCSO conduct the *Hubert* balancing test and identify, with respect to each specific category and document, the personal-privacy interest asserted and the basis for the conclusion that it outweighs the public interest in disclosure. Generalized assertions of § 552.102 are insufficient.
